Background

Dr. Horacio Ferriz has been acquiring data from four wells in Woodward Regional Park every three months since 2016 to measure possible contamination of groundwater from the campsite wastewater. Two wells check the composition of incoming groundwater and two wells sample for groundwater on the other side of the evaporation pond. We will develop a program to preprocess the data to compare the chemical analyses of both inbound and outbound groundwater.

Water Wells

This map shows the system of wells and ponds used to monitor and deal with waste water from the woodward reservoir recreation area. The blue line shows the direction of groundwater flow, which follows elevation gradient from high to low making it a gravity dependent flow. Wells are placed in a way that we can measure the total dissolved solids in the ground water before and after it passes under the evaporation pond which is the last step of wastewater sanitation for this site. Total Dissolved Solids we are concerned about are compounds like nitrates, carbonates, sulfates, and bicarbonates as well as elements like iron, potassium, calcium, magnesium, and sodium.

Evaporation Pond

Here we have a model of water penetrating through sediment layers and what a typical aquifer looks like. We have a shale layer that acts as an impermeable layer for the water, so the water cannot go deeper than that layer. This model shows how runoff water from a precipitation event as well as the natural flow of the groundwater towards a nearby river which is why it is important to keep possible contamination monitored. There is a sampling well before the flow reaches the evaporation pond, and a few wells after the evaporation pond, samples taken at all these wells will show whether or not there is a change of concentration of chemical species dissolved in the water. It will basically show if the groundwater is picking up anything when as it passes underneath the evaporation pond that we would not want for environmental purposes.
